Local Products

Sukang Iloko

Sukang Iloko (Ilocos Vinegar) A native vinegar made from fermented sugarcane juice. It has a strong, tangy flavor and is often used in Ilocano dishes like pinakbet or as a dip for longganisa and empanada.

Price: P180

Vigan Empanada

Vigan Empanada A local version of the empanada, filled with grated green papaya, mung beans, egg, and Vigan longganisa, all wrapped in a crispy rice-flour shell. It’s deep-fried and often enjoyed with Sukang Iloko.

Price: P85

Tinubong

A native delicacy made of glutinous rice, coconut milk, sugar, and other ingredients, cooked inside a bamboo tube over charcoal. It is often sold during festivals and is associated with traditional Ilocano cuisine

Price: P120

Kalamay

A sweet sticky rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and brown sugar, often wrapped in banana leaves. It’s a popular dessert or snack in Ilocos Sur during fiestas and celebrations.

Price: P100

Bagnet

A famous Ilocano delicacy of deep-fried pork belly that is crispy on the outside and tender inside. It’s often served with rice and is a staple in Ilocos cuisine.

Price: P300

Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a famous Ilocano vegetable dish made from native vegetables such as ampalaya, eggplant, okra, and string beans, usually cooked with bagoong. These vegetables are grown locally and are staple products in the region.

Price: P80

Ilocos Sur Handicrafts

Various handmade crafts including baskets, hats, and décor made from natural materials like buri palm, rattan, and bamboo, reflecting the artistry and cultural heritage of the local people.

Price: P200

Balikutsa

A traditional Ilocano sweet made from melted sugarcane molasses that is stretched and coiled into a white, sticky, chewy candy. It's often sold during festivals or in pasalubong centers.

Price: P80

Tobacco Products

Ilocos Sur is one of the traditional tobacco-growing provinces in the Philippines. Locally grown tobacco is used in making cigars and dried leaves for chewing or ceremonial purposes.

Price: P150

Cornick

Similar to chichacorn, but often crunchier and flavored differently. Cornick from Ilocos Sur is often seasoned with garlic, salt, or vinegar and is a popular snack.

Price: P80

Ukoy

A crispy Ilocano delicacy made from small shrimp mixed with grated vegetables (like squash or sweet potato), battered and deep-fried. Often served with sukang Iloko.

Price: P50

Binatog

A local street food made from boiled white corn kernels topped with grated coconut and sugar (or salt). It's a simple but beloved snack in Ilocano communities.

Price: P20
